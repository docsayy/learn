Acid–Base Workflow Wizard Nephrology / Critical Care

Added July 13, 2026 • Adult bedside calculator

Stepwise interpretation in the original workflow: blood gas → compensation → anion gap → HAGMA or NAGMA → GOLDMARK / HARDUP → delta analysis → structured assessment and plan.

Clinical safety: Use values obtained at approximately the same time. Venous pH and bicarbonate are often useful for metabolic assessment, but venous PCO₂ is not interchangeable with arterial PCO₂, and venous PO₂ must not be used to assess oxygenation.

Step 1 ABG: Primary Disorder & Compensation

▼

Start here: define the primary disorder and whether respiratory compensation is appropriate.

Required: pH, PCO₂, blood-gas HCO₃⁻ PO₂/FiO₂ is interpreted only for an arterial sample

Step 2 Anion Gap & Corrected Gap

▲

Use BMP and albumin to classify normal vs high anion gap and adjust for hypoalbuminemia.

AG = Na⁺ − (Cl⁻ + serum HCO₃⁻) Uses blood-gas HCO₃⁻ only when serum total CO₂ is unavailable

Step 3 HAGMA vs NAGMA Routing

▲

Based on corrected AG and presence of metabolic acidosis, decide whether this is primarily a high anion gap metabolic acidosis (HAGMA) or a normal anion gap metabolic acidosis (NAGMA).

Run Steps 1 and 2 to auto-classify.
HAGMA → GOLDMARK NAGMA → HARDUP

Step 4A HAGMA – GOLDMARK Causes

▲

Check what is present or suspected. If labs not yet available, tick “suspected/possible” boxes and use this as a prompt for workup.

Step 4B NAGMA – HARDUP Causes

▲

For normal anion gap metabolic acidosis, tick likely contributors and use this as a checklist for workup.

Step 5 Delta Gap & Delta–Delta

▲

For high anion gap metabolic acidosis, compare ΔAG with ΔHCO₃⁻ to look for a second metabolic process (additional non-gap acidosis or metabolic alkalosis).

ΔAG = corrected AG − entered normal AG ΔHCO₃⁻ = 24 − serum HCO₃⁻ Δ/Δ = ΔAG ÷ ΔHCO₃⁻
