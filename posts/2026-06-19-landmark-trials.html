Diabetes

Trial Major Clinical Pearl
ACCORD A1c target below 6% increased mortality compared with less intensive control.
EMPA-REG SGLT2 inhibitors reduce cardiovascular events and improve heart failure outcomes.
LEADER GLP-1 receptor agonists reduce cardiovascular events.
UKPDS / ADVANCE Intensive glucose control reduces microvascular but not macrovascular complications.
NICE-SUGAR ICU glucose target should generally be 140–180 mg/dL.
Key point: Microvascular benefits are real. Extremely aggressive glucose control is not.

Hypertension

Trial Takeaway
ALLHAT Established thiazides, ACE inhibitors, and calcium channel blockers as first-line therapy.
ACCOMPLISH ACE inhibitor plus calcium channel blocker outperformed ACE inhibitor plus HCTZ.
PATHWAY-2 Spironolactone is preferred fourth-line therapy for resistant hypertension.
ACCORD-BP SBP <120 did not improve outcomes in diabetics.
SPRINT SBP <120 improved outcomes in non-diabetics.

Modern guidelines generally target a blood pressure below 130/80 mmHg for most patients.

Atrial Fibrillation

AFFIRM

Rate control and rhythm control had similar outcomes in non-valvular atrial fibrillation. This trial established rate control as a reasonable strategy.

RACE II

Lenient rate control below 110 bpm was non-inferior to strict rate control below 80 bpm.

EAST-AFNET 4

Earlier rhythm control may improve cardiovascular outcomes compared with delayed rhythm control.

Resident pearl: Know AFFIRM and RACE II extremely well. They influence everyday atrial fibrillation management.

Heart Failure

Therapy Landmark Trials

Beta Blockers MERIT-HF, COPERNICUS, CIBIS-II
MRAs RALES, EMPHASIS-HF, EPHESUS
ARNI PARADIGM-HF
SGLT2 Inhibitors DAPA-HF
ICD MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT
CRT RAFT

FAIR-HF

IV iron improves symptoms, exercise capacity, and quality of life in iron-deficient HFrEF patients.

DAPA-HF

SGLT2 inhibitors improve heart failure outcomes even in patients without diabetes.

ARDS & Critical Care

Trial Clinical Impact
ARDSNet Low tidal volume ventilation, usually 6 mL/kg predicted body weight, reduces mortality.
PROSEVA Prone positioning improves survival in severe ARDS.
ACURASYS Suggested benefit of early paralysis.
ROSE Failed to confirm mortality benefit of routine paralysis.
FLORALI HFNC outperformed non-invasive ventilation in acute hypoxemic respiratory failure.
ABC Trial Daily sedation interruption reduces ventilator days and ICU stay.

Acute Coronary Syndrome

Trial Clinical Pearl
PLATO Ticagrelor is superior to clopidogrel in ACS.
VA Cooperative Study Aspirin reduces mortality in MI and unstable angina.
AVOID Avoid routine oxygen in non-hypoxic STEMI patients.
COLCOT Colchicine may reduce cardiovascular events after MI.

Sepsis & Shock

Trial Practice Change
ARISE / ProCESS / ProMISe Ended routine early goal-directed therapy with mandatory central lines and ScvO₂ monitoring.
SOAP II Norepinephrine preferred over dopamine.
VASST Vasopressin is mainly catecholamine-sparing.
ATHOS-3 Angiotensin II effective in refractory vasodilatory shock.
SMART Balanced crystalloids outperform normal saline in ICU patients.
SEPSISPAM MAP target 65–70 generally adequate.
PRORATA Procalcitonin useful for stopping antibiotics, not starting them.
BICAR-ICU Bicarbonate may help severe acidosis with AKI.

Steroids in Septic Shock

Annane, ADRENAL, CORTICUS, APROCCHSS, and related trials suggest steroids may accelerate shock reversal and may provide modest mortality benefit in selected patients with severe septic shock.

Transfusion Medicine

Trial Takeaway
TRICC Restrictive transfusion strategy supports a hemoglobin threshold near 7 g/dL in many critically ill patients.
TRISS Supports hemoglobin threshold of 7 g/dL in septic shock.
Upper GI Bleeding Trial Restrictive transfusion strategy reduces mortality.
High-yield rule: For most hospitalized patients, transfuse when hemoglobin falls below 7 g/dL.

COPD

REDUCE Trial

Five days of corticosteroids for COPD exacerbation is non-inferior to fourteen days.

Remember: Five days of steroids is usually enough for COPD exacerbation.

Cancer-Associated VTE

Trial Key Finding
SELECT-D Rivaroxaban reduces recurrent VTE but increases bleeding risk.
CLOT LMWH became standard therapy for cancer-associated thrombosis.

GI malignancy patients generally have higher bleeding risk and often remain better candidates for LMWH.

Bottom Line Takeaways

  1. Know AFFIRM and RACE II for atrial fibrillation.
  2. Know ARDSNet and PROSEVA for critical care.
  3. Know ACCORD and NICE-SUGAR for diabetes.
  4. Know SPRINT and ALLHAT for hypertension.
  5. Know DAPA-HF and PARADIGM-HF for heart failure.
  6. Know SMART, SOAP II, and PRORATA for ICU medicine.
  7. Know TRICC and TRISS for transfusion thresholds.
  8. Know REDUCE for COPD exacerbations.
  9. Know SELECT-D for cancer-associated VTE.
Resident survival rule: If you only memorize a handful of studies before intern year, start with AFFIRM, RACE II, ARDSNet, PROSEVA, ACCORD, NICE-SUGAR, SPRINT, ALLHAT, DAPA-HF, SMART, PRORATA, TRICC, and REDUCE. These trials repeatedly influence daily inpatient decision making and appear frequently on rounds, board exams, and teaching sessions.
