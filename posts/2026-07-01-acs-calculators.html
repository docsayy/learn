Quick Reminders
  • Primary PCI is preferred when it can be performed quickly by an experienced team.
  • Many pathways favor PCI if door-to-balloon is within about 90 minutes, or about 120 minutes from first medical contact.
  • Fibrinolysis may be considered if STEMI is within 12 hours of onset, PCI is delayed, and there are no contraindications.
  • Cardiogenic shock or severe heart failure generally favors PCI rather than lytics if possible.
  • Always check absolute and relative contraindications before fibrinolysis.
