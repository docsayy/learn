Infectious Disease Pearls

Cryptococcal Meningitis: How Much CSF Should Be Removed?

When treating elevated intracranial pressure from cryptococcal meningitis, therapeutic lumbar punctures may be required to lower opening pressure.

Remove approximately 20–30 mL of CSF when attempting to lower opening pressure.
  • Serial lumbar punctures may be needed daily.
  • Continue until the opening pressure is less than approximately 25 cm H₂O.
  • Once controlled, lumbar punctures may be spaced every 48–72 hours.
  • Cryptococcal meningitis and coccidioidal meningitis can both cause markedly elevated opening pressures.

Can Advanced HIV or MAC Cause Pancytopenia?

Yes. Disseminated Mycobacterium avium complex, or MAC, can invade the bone marrow and impair normal hematopoiesis.

  • Can reduce red blood cell production
  • Can reduce white blood cell production
  • Can reduce platelet production
  • May present as pancytopenia in advanced HIV

Liver Transplant Recipient on Lamivudine Despite Negative HBV Serologies

A liver transplant recipient may receive hepatitis B prophylaxis even if their own hepatitis B serologies are negative.

This can happen when the donor organ was hepatitis B positive.

Endocrine Pearls

First Branch Point in Cushing Syndrome

The first key step in evaluating Cushing syndrome is determining whether it is ACTH-independent or ACTH-dependent.

Pattern Common Causes
ACTH-independent Adrenal adenoma, adrenal carcinoma, exogenous steroids
ACTH-dependent Pituitary adenoma, ectopic ACTH production

High-Dose Dexamethasone Suppression Test

In ACTH-dependent Cushing syndrome, high-dose dexamethasone can help distinguish pituitary disease from ectopic ACTH production.

Response Interpretation
Cortisol suppresses Suggests pituitary adenoma
No suppression Suggests ectopic ACTH production, classically small cell lung cancer
Suppression points toward pituitary disease. No suppression points toward ectopic ACTH production.

Reactive Hypoglycemia After a Large Carbohydrate Meal

A benign cause of hypoglycemia after eating a large carbohydrate meal is reactive hypoglycemia, also called postprandial hypoglycemia.

  • Can occur after large carbohydrate loads
  • May be seen after gastric bypass
  • May cause syncope-like episodes if severe

Holding SGLT2 Inhibitors and Hypoglycemia After Gastric Bypass

In some gastric bypass patients, holding an SGLT2 inhibitor may increase risk for postprandial hypoglycemia.

The proposed mechanism is that stopping the SGLT2 inhibitor allows a higher glucose spike after a carbohydrate load, followed by an exaggerated insulin response and subsequent hypoglycemia.

Pulmonary and Dyspnea Pearls

The Top of the Dyspnea Pyramid

When thinking through dyspnea, pulmonary and cardiac causes form the broad base of the differential. At the top are the “three A’s.”

The three A’s at the top of the dyspnea pyramid are: Anemia, Acidosis, and Anxiety.
  • Pulmonary causes
  • Cardiac causes
  • Anemia
  • Acidosis
  • Anxiety
  • Bad blood, such as carbon monoxide poisoning or methemoglobinemia

Dapsone and Methemoglobinemia

Dapsone can cause methemoglobinemia and may present with unexplained hypoxemia.

Think of methemoglobinemia when pulse oximetry is low but the clinical picture or oxygen response does not make sense.

Hematology Pearls

Hydroxyurea in Sickle Cell Disease

Hydroxyurea can worsen cytopenias, including anemia. If a patient with sickle cell disease develops worsening anemia after dose escalation, hydroxyurea toxicity should be considered.

  • Hydroxyurea often increases MCV.
  • MCV greater than 100 can suggest medication effect/adherence.
  • Worsening anemia may require dose reduction.
  • Consider waiting until hemoglobin is stable above 7 g/dL and reticulocyte count is adequate before uptitrating.

VEXAS Syndrome

VEXAS is a rare adult-onset autoinflammatory and hematologic disorder.

VEXAS stands for vacuoles, E1 enzyme, X-linked, autoinflammatory, somatic.
  • Can cause fevers
  • Can cause skin findings
  • Can cause bone marrow failure
  • May require corticosteroids, immunosuppression, or bone marrow transplant in selected cases

Hypogammaglobulinemia from Long-Term Rituximab

Long-term rituximab therapy can cause hypogammaglobulinemia.

  • Monitor IgG levels periodically.
  • Patients may be at increased infection risk.
  • Consider immunology or hematology input if recurrent infections occur.

GI and Hepatology Pearls

Can Gallstone Obstruction Cause LFTs in the Thousands?

Yes. Acute biliary obstruction from a gallstone can sometimes cause dramatic transaminase elevation, even into the thousands.

Primary Biliary Cholangitis: Fatigue May Be the Main Symptom

Primary biliary cholangitis is classically associated with pruritus and cholestatic liver tests, but many patients may present with fatigue rather than classic itching or jaundice.

Iron Studies in Acute Liver Injury

Iron studies are indicated in the initial evaluation of acute liver injury because hemochromatosis is relatively common and can be missed if not checked.

Nodular Regenerative Hyperplasia

Nodular regenerative hyperplasia can cause nodular liver morphology and portal hypertension without classic cirrhosis.

Feature Nodular Regenerative Hyperplasia
Pathology Numerous small regenerative nodules
Fibrosis No extensive fibrosis, unlike cirrhosis
Clinical consequence Can cause portal hypertension
Associations Autoimmune disease, myeloproliferative disorders, medications, recurrent infections, solid organ transplant

Severe Acute Alcoholic Hepatitis and Liver Transplant

Some patients admitted with severe acute alcoholic hepatitis may still be considered for liver transplantation, especially if it is their first episode and they have not previously received counseling about alcohol cessation.

Repeated admissions after counseling about alcohol-related liver disease may make transplant candidacy less likely.

Rheumatology Pearls

Diffuse Alveolar Hemorrhage from Vasculitis

For vasculitis with diffuse alveolar hemorrhage, immunosuppression is essential, but it may take time to reduce pathogenic antibodies.

Plasmapheresis may be discussed in severe cases because it can rapidly remove circulating antibodies and immunoglobulins.

The role of plasmapheresis depends on the clinical scenario and evidence is mixed. This should involve specialist discussion with rheumatology, nephrology, and critical care.

EGPA: ANCA Serologies Are Not Very Sensitive

Eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis, or EGPA, is less commonly ANCA-positive than other ANCA-associated vasculitides.

Only about 30–40% of EGPA cases are MPO/p-ANCA positive.

IgG4-Related Disease and Retro-Orbital Proptosis

IgG4-related disease is a fibroinflammatory condition that can affect many organ systems and may present with retro-orbital disease or proptosis.

Avacopan Mechanism

Avacopan is used in ANCA-associated vasculitis as a steroid-sparing therapy.

Avacopan blocks the C5a receptor.

Neurology and Procedure Pearls

Internal Venous Plexus Bleeding During Lumbar Puncture

Significant bleeding during lumbar puncture may occur if the internal venous plexus is punctured.

  • The venous plexus is near the spinal canal.
  • Repeated redirection can increase the risk of vascular puncture.
  • If redirecting, pull the needle back close to the skin before changing angle.
  • Many small venous bleeds tamponade spontaneously if there are no neurologic symptoms.

Neurostorming After Traumatic Brain Injury

Traumatic brain injury can lead to recurrent episodes of excessive sympathetic activity, now often called paroxysmal sympathetic hyperactivity.

Feature Possible Findings
Autonomic signs Fever, hypertension, tachycardia, tachypnea
Trigger Often induced by benign stimulation
Course Starts rapidly and resolves after a variable period
Clinical importance Can persist or worsen if untreated

Abdominal Pain Framework

Four Buckets for Abdominal Pain

A useful clinical reasoning framework divides abdominal pain into four major categories.

Bucket Examples
Life-threatening causes Bowel perforation, mesenteric ischemia, small bowel obstruction
Intra-abdominal causes GI tract and abdominal organ pathology
Extra-abdominal causes Ovarian torsion, kidney stone, cardiovascular or pulmonary causes
Imaging-negative causes Metabolic, medications, toxins, functional pain, other causes

Imaging-Negative Abdominal Pain

When imaging is unrevealing, consider:

  • Metabolic causes
  • Medications and toxins
  • Functional pain
  • Other less common causes

Angioedema Pearls

Mast Cell vs Bradykinin-Mediated Angioedema

Mast cell-mediated angioedema and bradykinin-mediated angioedema behave differently.

Feature Mast Cell-Mediated Bradykinin-Mediated
Speed Usually faster Usually slower
Urticaria Often present Absent
Trigger Often clear May be unclear
Examples Allergy, anaphylaxis, opioids, contrast ACE inhibitors, C1 inhibitor deficiency, hereditary angioedema
Bradykinin-mediated angioedema is slower. Remember: “brady” is slow.

Idiopathic Angioedema

Idiopathic angioedema typically does not cause respiratory or circulatory symptoms.

ACE Inhibitor Angioedema May Have No Clear Trigger

ACE inhibitor-induced angioedema can occur soon after starting therapy, after a dose change, or even years after stable use.

Can Angioedema Affect the Bowel Wall?

Yes. Angioedema can involve the bowel wall and cause colicky abdominal pain with bowel wall edema on imaging.

Acquired vs Hereditary C1 Inhibitor Deficiency

Condition Typical Presentation
Acquired C1 inhibitor deficiency Older patients
Hereditary angioedema Younger patients, often with family history

Take-Home Pearls

  • For cryptococcal meningitis with elevated pressure, remove about 20–30 mL of CSF and repeat LPs as needed.
  • MAC can invade bone marrow and cause pancytopenia in advanced HIV.
  • First branch point in Cushing syndrome is ACTH-dependent vs ACTH-independent.
  • Dapsone can cause methemoglobinemia.
  • Gallstone obstruction can cause marked transaminase elevation.
  • PBC may present mainly with fatigue.
  • Nodular regenerative hyperplasia can cause portal hypertension without cirrhosis.
  • EGPA is often ANCA-negative.
  • Avacopan blocks the C5a receptor.
  • Bradykinin angioedema is slower and usually lacks urticaria.
  • Neurostorming after TBI is paroxysmal sympathetic hyperactivity.
